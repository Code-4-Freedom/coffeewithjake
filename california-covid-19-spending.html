California COVID-19 Spending

Jake

If I were in politics...

Specifically if I was the governor of California*.

I would have prioritized hospital preparedness over vaccination encouragement.

What I mean by hospital preparedness is insuring that the hospitals had enough of the needed resources to treat individuals with COVID-19 like Hydroxycholoquine and ventilators. Hydroxycholoquine costs (on average) $148.95 (retail) and ventilators cost between $20,000 and $50,000 (For this post we are just going to assume the average cost of $35,000). 

Let's say the money spent on the vaccine lottery and given illegally to illegal immigrants was spent on hospital resources, that would give us a budget of approximately $191,000,000. With this money, we could purchase 1,275,595 doses of Hydroxycholoquine with $1,000,000 devoted to delivery costs or 5,428 ventilators (Once again with a $1,000,000 budget for delivering the units). The money could also be split between the two. 

However, the point is that the money spent illegally on people who are not here legally and on a lottery could have been spent in better ways to help provide better treatment for the people of California and be prepared for any future flare ups of the disease. With any disease, there are flare ups, or hills and valleys. Being prepared for the next hill should be a priority, and a vaccine does not do not. The vaccine has been proven to not protect the vaccinated from contacting the disease post receiving the vaccine, that and it is not FDA approved. The government should not encourage the use of a drug that is not FDA approved.
